Post Event

It is important to continue to operate even after the event is carried through. Create continuity and credibility by completing the post-event tasks immediately after the event. Post-event management includes thank you notes and feedback, photos and news, website, and network updates, analysing the event result, event reporting and financial management.

Evaluate and complete the event.

Post-event communication

Thank you note.

A thank you note is a great way to acknowledge all who contributed to the event and who experienced it. Send the thank you notes as soon as possible after the event, even on the same night, right after the event. Recipients may include guests, speakers, performers, event staff, sponsors, and collaborators.

Follow-up.

Continue the event lifecycle, impact, and guest engagement by sharing videos, trailers, webinars, and/or pictures on the web shortly after the event. Highlights may be shared on social media. Offer the material to your stakeholders as well. Decide on the date for next years' event and share it as an early reminder or send a save-the-date invitation.

Follow-up enquiries.

Agree who will respond to public enquiries after the event. Participants may ask and request additional information, such as the material presented.

Feedback and analysis

Feedback.

Decide whom you wish to have feedback from, whether it is participants, volunteers, working group, sponsors, donors etc. Create questionnaires on Teams for each group. Send the feedback form soon (one hour to one day) after the event while the event is still fresh in the attendee's mind. Set a deadline for when to answer by the latest.

Post-event session.

Conduct a team debrief to learn each other's thoughts. Ask the critical questions: Did we meet the objective of the event? Where did we succeed? What can we do to make it better next time? Cover the key functions: registration, set-up, programme, catering, signage/decor, guest relations, etc. Create a relaxed environment where people are comfortable sharing their thoughts to improve future events.

Event analysis.

Depending on how the event has been monitored, you are able to analyse the outcome by evaluating the number of attendees, feedback, budget, collaboration, media visibility, etc.

Documentation and reporting.

Finalise follow-up reports of the event production process and share your knowledge and networks with other members in your project team. By sharing best practices, we avoid having to start every event from scratch. Make future recommendations, preliminary plans, and reservations for the next event. Send report to .

Financial management

Invoice flow.

All bought services and suppliers should be informed about the invoicing details. All events and projects must have references (project code) and contact person (who also inspects the invoice). All payments should be done soon after the event. If some invoice is not received, send a reminder to the suppliers.

Staffing payment.

If you need to pay fees for hourly work, contact department administrator about processing the payment. Remember that they must be commenced and have provide a timesheet. Contact your faculty administrator to process them.

Financial reporting.

Contact finance before any expenses have occurred if a financial report is needed. This is especially important when reporting to an external partner or for auditing. Gather all receipts and documentation, final registration data, catering participants, etc. for reporting.

Budget update.

Confirm the events financial status, when all invoices have been processed, update the budget, and share it with the event owner.

VAT.

In IADT all cost calculation, and budgets are done excluding VAT, both income and expenses. Where the primary purpose of the event is strictly to provide education/vocational training only, the registration/admission fees are exempt events from charging VAT. .
